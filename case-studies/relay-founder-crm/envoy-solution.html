Executive Summary



Early-stage founders lose between 30-40% of warm leads — not because they don't care, but because the tools built to help them were never designed for how they actually work. Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho — powerful platforms built for dedicated sales teams — demand constant manual updates that founders simply don't have time to make.


Envoy is different. It is a fully managed AI sales agent platform that works silently across every channel a founder already uses — WhatsApp, Gmail, Telegram — capturing conversations, logging deals, and flagging what needs attention. No new apps. No manual entry. No CRM to maintain.


The founder just sells. Envoy handles everything else.


The Core Insight

 

Every existing CRM was built on one assumption: someone on your team will update the system.

 

Envoy is built on a different assumption: the founder has no time to update anything. So the system must update itself.


The Problem We Are Solving



How Founders Actually Sell

A typical early-stage founder manages 50-150 active conversations simultaneously across 4-6 different communication channels. They spend 70% of their time actively selling — closing, pitching, negotiating — and the remaining 30% on everything else that running a company demands.


There is no time left to log calls, update pipelines, or maintain a CRM. So they don't. And deals fall through the cracks.


Why Existing CRMs Fail Founders

The problem is not that existing CRMs lack features. The problem is that they were built for a completely different user — a dedicated sales team where updating the CRM is someone's full-time job.

The Intelligence Layer — How Envoy Knows What to Log



One of the hardest product design challenges in Envoy is knowing which conversations to log as sales activity and which to ignore completely. A founder's WhatsApp contains messages from their spouse, friends, co-founders, investors, and leads — all mixed together.


Envoy solves this through a four-layer classification system, designed around one core principle: high precision over high recall. It is better to occasionally miss a conversation than to pollute the CRM with noise.


The Four Classification Layers


Layer 1 — Known Leads (Automatic, Silent)

Any contact the founder has already confirmed as a lead is monitored automatically. Every message, voice note, and file exchanged with them is captured without the founder doing anything.


Layer 2 — New Contacts (AI Classification with Confidence Scoring)

When a message arrives from an unknown contact, Envoy's AI analyses it and assigns a confidence score based on commercial signals — pricing questions, product mentions, follow up language, inbound responses to outbound messages. Based on the score, Envoy takes one of three actions.

Layer 3 — Founder Override (Always In Control)

The founder can instruct Envoy at any time to always track or never track a specific contact, phone number, or email domain. These rules persist and override AI classification permanently.


Layer 4 — Dedicated Sales Channel (Premium Option)

For founders who want complete separation, Envoy provides a dedicated WhatsApp Business number. Leads contact this number. Envoy monitors it exclusively. The founder's personal WhatsApp is never touched. Every conversation on the sales number is a lead by definition — no classification needed.


How Envoy Gets Smarter Over Time

Every time the founder confirms or dismisses a classification, Envoy learns. Over weeks, it understands this specific founder's network — who their leads tend to be, what language indicates commercial intent in their industry, which contacts are personal. The number of confirmation requests drops significantly after the first two weeks.

Feature 1 — Focus Constraints



Because Envoy is built on powerful general-purpose agent infrastructure, it is technically capable of doing almost anything — booking flights, writing code, searching the web, managing files. Left unconstrained, founders would inevitably start using Envoy as a personal AI assistant rather than a dedicated sales agent.


This creates real problems: server costs become unpredictable, product positioning gets blurry, support becomes unmanageable, and the core sales intelligence that makes Envoy valuable gets diluted. Focus constraints solve all of this by design.


How Focus Constraints Work

Every Envoy instance is deployed with a locked system prompt — a set of instructions baked into the agent at the infrastructure level that defines exactly what Envoy will and will not do. The founder cannot override this. It is not a setting — it is the product.


What Envoy Will Do

 

Log and track sales conversations across connected channels

Manage and update the founder's deal pipeline

Send proactive follow up reminders and daily digests

Answer questions about active deals and lead history

Draft contextual sales messages and follow ups

Brief the founder before meetings with relevant lead context


What Envoy Will Not Do

 

Search the web for general queries

Book restaurants, travel, or handle personal tasks

Write code, answer technical questions, or act as a general assistant

Engage with topics unrelated to the founder's sales pipeline


How Envoy Handles Out-of-Scope Requests

When a founder asks Envoy to do something outside its defined scope, Envoy does not just refuse. It redirects warmly — and uses the moment to bring the founder back to something valuable.

Why This Is a Feature, Not a Limitation

Focus constraints are not a weakness — they are a deliberate product decision that makes Envoy better at its job. A tool that does everything is a tool that does nothing particularly well.


"Envoy does not try to be everything. It is the best sales agent in the world — nothing more, nothing less."
